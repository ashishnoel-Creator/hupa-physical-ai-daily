### Data collection

- **The Xense round is the purest capture-thesis confirmation of the window.** The company's newly released XTac UMI G1 is a wearable tactile data-collection gripper: a human wears it, does the task, and it records synchronized visuo-tactile interaction data without any robot in the loop. The raise is explicitly earmarked for "deploying and validating visuo-tactile data acquisition equipment and large-scale tactile datasets in real-world scenarios," alongside industrializing its tricolor optical tactile sensors and refining what it calls the only physical intelligence model with native tactile and contact support (VTLA, plus a tactile world model). 300+ clients since a May 2024 founding. Read with Tashan's TS-Echo finger cot from Monday: body-free tactile capture went from one company's product to a funded category in 48 hours. [Gasgoo](https://autonews.gasgoo.com/articles/news/seeds-xense-robotics-secures-100-million-yuan-funding-top-industrial-players-and-jide-electrical-enter-strategically-2077015745534672897)
- **Tsing Standard is the industrial-scale version of the same bet.** Eight years of closed-loop data work in autonomous driving and industrial inspection, now repositioned as an embodied AI data company: TsingLoop takes raw multi-source signals from real workstations (battery pack inspection, final assembly, underground mines), aligns them across time, space and semantics, and packages them as standardized, reusable data assets that can be replayed and recombined in simulation. The pitch is a "one foundation, one brain, a hundred vertical applications" data flywheel, and the buyer proof is 10,000+ shipped units across nearly every domestic automaker and battery maker. Validated capture against a spec, sold as infrastructure. [Gasgoo](https://autonews.gasgoo.com/articles/news/tsing-standard-completes-financing-worth-hundreds-of-millions-2077014834716389377)

### Embedded AI systems

- **LimX's FluxVLA Engine is the infrastructure story inside the funding story.** Positioned as an enterprise-level open-source platform, it covers the full pipeline from data processing through simulation training, real-robot iteration and hardware deployment, with the stated aim of moving the industry from "only a small number of teams master model training" to a low-barrier ecosystem. LimX's own architecture is a three-tier stack: System 0 whole-body motion foundation model, System 1 VLA/WAM, System 2 embodied agent OS (COSA) built on LLMs and world models. A body maker open-sourcing the training toolchain commoditizes everyone's pipeline except the data that flows through it. [36Kr](https://eu.36kr.com/en/p/3893976502287618)

### Research papers worth a look

*Note on coverage: the arXiv cs.RO listing still showed Monday July 13 as the newest batch at run time, so no fresh announcement day was available. Two Monday-batch papers that yesterday's brief did not cover clear the bar:*

- **Tactile and Vision Conditioned Contact-Centric Control for Whole-Arm Manipulation (arXiv 2607.09218, listed Monday; Cornell and Toyota Research Institute, Tapomayukh Bhattacharjee group, RSS 2026).** Whole-arm manipulation conditioned on both tactile and visual input, treating contact along the entire arm as the control primitive rather than just the fingertips. Lands the same week two Chinese tactile-capture companies raised: the models are starting to consume the modality the capture layer is racing to supply. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09218)
- **CLAP: Direct VLM-to-VLA Adaptation via Language-Action Grounding (arXiv 2607.08974, listed Monday; OMRON SINIC X).** Adapts a vision-language model directly into a VLA through language-action grounding instead of full action-data retraining. Every method that shortens the path from pretrained VLM to deployable VLA changes what kind of data the conversion step actually needs. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08974)
