    Research papers worth a look

    • GesVLA: Gesture-Aware Vision-Language-Action Model (arXiv 2605.22812, May 21). Two-stage training strategy that adds gesture perception to a VLA backbone. Result: gesture conditioning consistently improves target-grounding accuracy and human-robot interaction efficiency on multiple real-world manipulation tasks. The interesting part for our wedge: any data we capture that includes the human operator’s pointing / handing / showing gestures is now training fuel for a different loss term. The “what should we capture” rubric just got one bullet wider. arXiv
    • DreamDojo: A Generalist Robot World Model from Large-Scale Human Videos (NVIDIA, open-sourced Feb 2026, still the structural reference). 700M-parameter spatiotemporal Transformer trained on 44,711 hours of human-egocentric video across 6,015 tasks and 1.135M trajectories. 10.81 FPS real-time inference. Open weights, open code, open dataset. The bear-case reference for any headcam capture pitch in 2026. Read in detail. DreamDojo paper site, NVIDIA GitHub, VentureBeat
    • A Comprehensive Survey on World Models for Embodied AI (arXiv 2510.16732). Newest survey paper that explicitly maps world-model designs across the spectrum from pixel-space generative (DreamDojo, Genie 3) to latent-physics-grounded (Pelican-Unified, Hamiltonian world models). One-doc reference for getting current. arXiv
