### Data collection

- **"Do as I Do" is the capture signal of the day, because it treats everyday human video as the primary manipulation-data source and tells you how to collect it well.** Bhawna Paliwal, Haritheja Etukuru, Pieter Abbeel, Nur Muhammad Mahi Shafiullah and Jitendra Malik present an algorithm that reconstructs hand-object interactions from monocular RGB human video, both egocentric and exocentric, then retargets them into executable action sequences for multi-fingered dexterous hands. It beats prior state of the art on hand-object estimation and on extracting trajectories from raw clips, and the paper closes with an "efficacy playbook for practitioners collecting human data for manipulation." That last line is the part we care about. The constraint on human-video-to-robot data was never the supply of video, it was the embodiment gap and the quality of the reconstruction. A named playbook for collecting the right video is exactly the discipline the thesis keeps arguing wins. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19333)

### Data annotation & validation

- **HT-Bench is the fresh validation-side signal, a large-scale benchmark that pairs egocentric vision with full-hand tactile data and scores whether a tactile representation actually encodes contact geometry.** From a team led by Yuanxin Zhong, it carries 10M RGB frames and 7.8M tactile frames across 226 tasks, and it grades representations on four tasks: fine-grained tactile retrieval, masked tactile inpainting, vision-to-tactile synthesis, and multimodal frame prediction. Their HandTouch encoder lifts Recall@5 on tactile retrieval from 74.65% to 85.23% and cuts inpainting RMSE from 0.022 to 0.010. Read it next to RobOmni from yesterday. Two tactile benchmarks landing in the same week is the field admitting it cannot yet measure whether touch data is any good, which is the same measurement gap the audited-capture argument is built on. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19161)
- *No net-new public move from the named annotation vendors (Scale, Mercor, Surge, Labelbox, Snorkel, Sama, iMerit, Invisible, Centific, Toloka) cleared the window.*

### Research papers worth a look

- **Do as I Do: Dexterous Manipulation Data from Everyday Human Videos (Paliwal, Etukuru, Liang, Abbeel, Shafiullah, Malik), arXiv June 17.** Reconstructs hand-object interaction from monocular RGB human video, egocentric and exocentric, and retargets it to multi-fingered dexterous hands to produce robot-complete manipulation trajectories. Beats prior state of the art on hand-object estimation and trajectory extraction, and ships a practitioner playbook for collecting human manipulation data. The clearest recent statement that the bottleneck in human-video data is reconstruction and collection discipline, not video supply. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19333)
- **HT-Bench: Benchmarking and Learning Dexterous Full-Hand Tactile Representations with Egocentric Vision (Huang, Wu, Jiang et al.), arXiv June 17.** A 10M-RGB-frame, 7.8M-tactile-frame benchmark across 226 tasks that pairs egocentric vision with full-hand tactile data and grades whether a representation encodes real contact geometry. Introduces the HandTouch vision-tactile encoder, which improves tactile retrieval Recall@5 from 74.65% to 85.23%. Evaluation infrastructure for touch data, the same lesson RobOmni taught from the manipulation side. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19161)
