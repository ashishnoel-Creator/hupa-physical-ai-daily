### Data collection

- **A standardized reference embodiment is a quiet repricing of captured-demonstration data, and it cuts toward the HuPa wedge.** Until yesterday every lab collecting human demonstrations had to reason about which robot the data would map onto. With H2 Plus shipping as the common research platform to Ai2, ETH, Stanford and UCSD in October, demonstration data collected anywhere now has a single dominant academic target to retarget against. That lowers cross-embodiment friction, which is exactly the friction that used to make raw hours feel defensible. What survives the standardization is coverage and yield. Environments, hands and demographics that are not in any public corpus, captured against a published spec with an audit trail, not hour count on a generic rig. [NVIDIA newsroom](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-open-humanoid-robot-reference-design)

### Embedded AI systems

- **Jetson Thor shipping specs are now firm, not previewed. 2,070 FP4 TFLOPs, 7.5x the compute of Jetson Orin, 3.5x better energy efficiency, 40-130W configurable.** This is the onboard inference engine inside H2 Plus, so the reference research humanoid and the reference edge compute ship as one bundle in October. The same Blackwell-at-the-edge architecture scales up into the N1X laptop SoC that NVIDIA formally announced (20-core ARM CPU, integrated Blackwell GPU at 6,144 CUDA cores, up to 128GB LPDDR5X, 180-200 AI TOPS) and DGX Station coming to Windows for trillion-parameter local inference. The twist is that NVIDIA just stapled its edge silicon onto Chinese robot hardware, so the China-domestic-chip wedge now competes against a stack that has already absorbed the Chinese chassis. ### Robotics & humanoids

### Research papers worth a look

- **TAVIS: A Benchmark for Egocentric Active Vision and Anticipatory Gaze in Imitation Learning (arXiv 2605.07943).** Evaluation infrastructure for active-vision imitation learning, with task suites on two humanoid-torso embodiments (GR1T2, Reachy2). The relevant angle for a capture-side wedge is that it formalizes anticipatory gaze and active vision as things a dataset has to carry, not just hand and object trajectories. Where the camera looks and when it looks there becomes part of the spec. That is a yield-and-validation argument, not an hour-count one, and it is the direction the egocentric-capture literature keeps pushing. [arXiv 2605.07943](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07943)
- **NVIDIA Isaac GR00T Reference Humanoid Robot (open release, June 1).** Not a paper, but the open hardware-plus-software reference design is the release that matters most this week. H2 Plus chassis plus Sharpa hands plus Jetson Thor plus the open Isaac GR00T platform, available October, adopted by Ai2, ETH Zurich, Stanford Robotics Center and UCSD ARCL. Track it as the embodiment that the next wave of academic VLA results will be benchmarked on. [NVIDIA newsroom](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-open-humanoid-robot-reference-design)
