### Research papers worth a look

- **Any2Any (arXiv 2605.23733, May 25 via Tao Yu).** Efficient cross-embodiment transfer for humanoid whole-body tracking. The claim: with only 1% compute / data, the team can transfer Gear-Sonic policies from Unitree G1 to LimX Oli and Luna with fast convergence and strong tracking. SONIC (NVIDIA) as the foundation model, LoRA adapters as the transfer mechanism. Worth reading because the academic argument here is that the embodiment-specific data bottleneck for humanoid policies can be sidestepped with very small adapter cheques on top of a shared foundation model. If that holds at field scale, every per-embodiment data-collection program just got measured against the LoRA-adapter cost curve. Direct relevance to our captured-data wedge - any captured dataset that is portable across embodiments via SONIC + LoRA is more defensible than one that locks to a single OEM. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23733)
- **HumanEgo (arXiv 2605.24934, May 26).** Zero-shot robot learning from minutes of human egocentric video. Hardware-agnostic, robot-data-free pipeline. The thesis: a robot manipulation policy can be learned from minutes of raw human egocentric video without any robot-specific data. Companion to EgoExo-WM and Being-H0.7 in the broader egocentric-video-as-pretraining thesis. Read the failure-mode section before the next investor pitch on rig spec. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2605.24934)
- **EgoAction (arXiv 2605.24496, May 26).** Egocentric action composition with reliability-aware temporal fusion for the EPIC-KITCHENS action-detection challenge at CVPR 2026. Cleaner published baseline on egocentric action-detection metrics that any HuPa-captured kitchen / household dataset can be measured against. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24496)
