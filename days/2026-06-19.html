    Data collection

    • GAM is a collection signal dressed as a model result, because its whole premise is that the geometry has to be in the data, not reconstructed from flat frames. The paper’s complaint is that VLAs and video world-action models “operate primarily on 2D image frames or 2D-derived latent spaces, leaving implicit the 3D geometry required for contact-rich manipulation.” Read that as a spec demand. If the field moves toward geometry-native policies, the value of a capture rig shifts toward whoever records clean depth, force and multi-view geometry at source rather than whoever logs the most RGB hours. That is a harder rig and a tighter QC bar, which is the direction our own sensor spec (global shutter, 9-axis IMU, split head-and-waist architecture) is already pointed. arXiv 2606.17046

    Embedded AI systems

    • GAM doubles as the embedded read today, and the number that matters is 55×. Splitting the geometric foundation model at an intermediate layer and reusing the shallow layers as the observation encoder is an architecture choice aimed squarely at the edge-latency problem the WAM cluster keeps hitting: video-action denoising is too slow to run a body in real time. A 55× speedup over foundation-scale baselines is the kind of number that decides whether a policy runs on-device or stays in the cloud. The on-device-inference race is being run on architecture, not just silicon. arXiv 2606.17046

    Research papers worth a look

    • Geometric Action Model for Robot Policy Learning (KAIST AI, ETH Zurich), arXiv 2606.17046, June 15. A language-conditioned manipulation policy that repurposes a pretrained geometric foundation model (GFM) as a shared substrate for perception, temporal prediction and action decoding. It splits the GFM at an intermediate layer: shallow layers act as the observation encoder, and a causal future predictor inserted at the split forecasts future latent tokens conditioned on language, proprioception and action history. Reported more accurate, more robust, and 55× faster than foundation-model-scale baselines. Read it for two reasons. The thesis is that 2D-frame VLAs and video WAMs leave the 3D geometry of contact-rich tasks implicit, and that owning geometry as the native representation is both better and far cheaper to run. For a capture business that is a spec argument: the moat moves toward whoever records real 3D geometry, depth and force at source, not whoever logs the most flat video. arXiv 2606.17046, project page
