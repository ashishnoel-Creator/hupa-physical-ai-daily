    Data annotation & validation

    • The Deedy Das market map is the annotation-market document of the window, and its concentration number is the one to keep. His July 12 tally counts 50+ companies selling training data and RL environments to the big labs, totaling roughly $8.5B in revenue and $100B in valuation, with over 75% of both sitting in four names: Scale, Surge, Mercor and Handshake. Two reads for this column. First, an $8.5B revenue pool confirms data supply is a real industry, not a services footnote, and the market is paying roughly 12x revenue for it. Second, a four-player oligopoly at the top means the open field is not “compete with Scale on volume,” it is the segments the big four do not hold, and physical-world capture with validation is conspicuously not where their revenue sits today. RL environments getting equal billing with data in the framing is also worth noting: the labs are increasingly buying places for models to practice, not just examples to imitate. X

    Robotics & humanoids

    • The ISO 10218:2025 readiness gap is the sleeper strategic story of the weekend. Interact Analysis interviewed robot manufacturers on the updated industrial-robot safety standard and found a three-tier split: established global vendors are prepared, midsize suppliers have significant awareness gaps, and emerging Asian suppliers, particularly Chinese companies expanding into Europe, are notably less prepared, with compliance R&D costs “significantly underestimated.” The standard becomes mandatory for CE-marked products when Machinery Regulation EU 2023/1230 replaces the old Machinery Directive on January 20, 2027, pending OJEU listing that historically takes a year plus. Read against Anyverse Dynamics clearing full-domain CE certification first (covered July 11, no change): the sharpest Chinese players treat European compliance as an offensive weapon while the rest of the export wave has not priced it in. Whoever holds the paperwork holds the market. The Robot Report
