### Embedded AI systems

- **The Gasgoo chip piece is the embedded read of the day, and its core claim is that the whole industry is measuring the wrong thing.** The dominant narrative is the compute race: Jetson Thor at 2,070 FP4 TFLOPS, Qualcomm's Yuelong IQ10 at 700 TOPS, Black Sesame's A2000X at an equivalent 1,000 TOPS. The argument is that cloud inference chases throughput while a robot executing a precise grasp needs latency determinism, a stable and predictable response time per command, which does not show up in a TOPS figure at all. Take tactile feedback: the force signal has to round-trip from sensor to joint motor in milliseconds or the robot drops or crushes the object, and that interrupt-latency budget is invisible in the headline compute number. This is splitting the hardware into a "big brain" (perception, planning, multimodal, heavy compute, relaxed timing) and a "cerebellum" (motion control and real-time feedback, light compute, extreme latency and power demands). On the brain side NVIDIA's Halos for Robotics now bundles chips, OS and safety certification with 43 companies signed on. On the cerebellum side the domestic firms have an opening, because those chips sit closer to MCUs on mature nodes: CAS Wireless Semiconductor's analog-architecture cerebellum ASIC claims to drop key control-command latency from 2.8ns to 0.2ns, and its April CT-21X is billed as China's first dedicated GaN magnetic-encoding chip for humanoid limb control. The read for us is that the embedded stack is bifurcating, and the layer where capture and contact data actually matter is the real-time one nobody can fake with raw TOPS. [Gasgoo](https://autonews.gasgoo.com/articles/news/44-billion-yuan-floods-into-embodied-ai-yet-the-chip-sector-has-an-uncharted-territory-2071587973328846848)
- **The in-house-chip move got two concrete data points in June, both about OEMs trying to own the silicon: UBTECH and Maxxiri formed a JV, Xixuan Chuangzhi, targeting tape-out in H2 2027 and mass production in 2028, and Li Auto shipped its self-developed Mahe M100 while calling the car an "embodied AI terminal."** UBTECH's stated driver is cost: chairman Zhou Jian says foreign chips and materials run about one-third of a humanoid's BOM, and that general-purpose silicon is not optimized for robots. The counter, from Intel Labs China's Song Jiqiang, is that the robot market is still too small to make dedicated chips profitable and VLA is too unsettled to lock in silicon. Whether dedicated wins turns on whether VLA and world models converge in the next three to five years. For a capture layer the relevant part is that the BOM pressure is pushing the whole stack toward domestic, vertically-integrated hardware, which is the same direction the data layer is moving. [Gasgoo](https://autonews.gasgoo.com/articles/news/44-billion-yuan-floods-into-embodied-ai-yet-the-chip-sector-has-an-uncharted-territory-2071587973328846848)
