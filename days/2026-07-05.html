    Robotics & humanoids

    • The SKF and Leaderdrive JV is the hardware signal of the day, and it lands on the exact layer the China column keeps flagging as the constraint: the joint. SKF takes a 60% majority, the venture is headquartered in China to sit close to the supply chain and the customers, and it builds high-precision transmission components (the harmonic-reducer and precision-gear class of part) for robot joints that have to survive continuous industrial duty cycles. Operational by end of 2026, with SKF’s global sales network used to push into Europe, Japan and the US on top of the Chinese market. Two reads. First, this is the actuator bottleneck getting Western industrial capital two days after Quanzhibo’s joint-module A+++ and Cang Yu’s “the hands are lagging” keynote, so the money is agreeing with the founders in real time. Second, the direction of travel is telling. A Swedish incumbent is taking majority control of a Chinese-headquartered venture to get access to the fastest-growing humanoid market, which is the mirror image of the usual “Western OEM cannot price into Asia” framing. On the hardware supply chain, the gravity is pulling the other way. PR Newswire, Gasgoo
    • Lexiang Tech is the consumer-robot signal, and the tell is who wrote the lead check. Ant Group led, with Geely Capital and 37 Interactive Entertainment (a gaming company) and Yuanhe Puhua joining and existing backer Monolith following on oversubscribed. The company launched a home-embodied-intelligence brand, “Zeroth,” and claims a full-stack build across body structure, embodied general models, VLA models, joint modules, lightweight arms and what it calls consciousness-mimicking models. The number that matters for the thesis is not the 30,000 orders or the 600% revenue jump, it is the stated method: Lexiang says it finds product-market fit through high-frequency user testing and real-world data, not intuition. A consumer-robot company treating in-home usage as its data engine is the demand side of the capture argument, and the environments it is instrumenting are Chinese living rooms. Gasgoo
    • Adjacent, logged not led: Agility Robotics’ June 24 SPAC deal with Churchill Capital Corp XI at a $2.5B pre-money value, ticker AGLT, positioned as the only US-listed pure-play humanoid with active commercial deployments. Eleven days old and outside the freshness window, noted here once because it did not get a pointer in the earlier briefs and it is the cleanest US public-market humanoid comp now on the board. The Robot Report

    China spotlight

    • Two July 2 moves, one theme: capital is buying the parts of the stack the founders admitted were the bottleneck, and it is doing it fast. SKF took majority control of a Chinese joint-component venture (the actuator layer) and Ant Group led a home-robot round (the consumer-deployment-as-data-engine layer). Read against the July 3 Gasgoo conference, where Cang Yu called data and hands the two unsolved problems, this is the money moving to match the diagnosis inside 48 hours. The environments stay domestic in both cases, Chinese factories for SKF’s industrial humanoids and Chinese homes for Lexiang, and the one thing neither round claims to have solved is where clean, grounded, audit-trailed real-world data comes from at scale. That gap is still open, and it is still not being filled from India. SKF via Gasgoo, Lexiang via Gasgoo
