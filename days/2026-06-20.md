### Data annotation & validation

- **SC3-Eval is the validation story of the day, and it reframes evaluation itself as a data problem.** Evaluating generalist manipulation policies in the real world is slow and expensive, so the field wants to simulate rollouts with action-conditioned video world models. The catch is those rollouts drift, lose multi-camera coherence, and fail on policies outside the training set. SC3-Eval's fix is three forms of self-consistency, the load-bearing one being forward-inverse dynamics consistency, which trains the model to both predict frames from actions and recover actions from frames, anchoring generated rollouts to a physically plausible action manifold. It hits a 0.929 closed-loop Pearson correlation and a 0.119 MMRV across seven real-world VLA policies, beating three prior video-model baselines, and it reproduces the actual failure modes policies show in the real world rather than just ranking them. The honest read for a data business: even a video-model evaluator earns its accuracy by being tied back to real action data, so the demand does not leave real capture, it moves toward whoever has the ground-truth rollouts to anchor and calibrate the simulator. [arXiv 2606.18610](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18610), [project page](https://weichengtseng.github.io/sc3-eval/)
- **Built's pipeline is the other validation read, because the partnership is explicitly about labeling edge cases, not collecting volume.** The xLAB writeup describes "systematically collecting and labeling edge cases" to push an edge AI personnel-detection model toward catching transient dangers humans miss. That is annotation framed as a safety-validation function, which is exactly the bundled, audited capture-plus-label position the thesis says carries the margin. [The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/xlab-and-built-robotics-partner-to-advance-construction/)

### Research papers worth a look

- **SC3-Eval: Evaluating Robot Foundation Models via Self-Consistent Video Generation (Physical Intelligence + NVIDIA), arXiv 2606.18610, submitted June 17.** A recipe that turns a pretrained video foundation model into a policy evaluator by enforcing three forms of consistency. Forward-inverse dynamics consistency jointly predicts frames from actions and recovers actions from frames, anchoring rollouts to a physically plausible action manifold. Cross-view consistency inpaints each camera view from the other to keep multi-camera observations coherent over long rollouts with no explicit memory. Test-time consistency reuses the inverse-dynamics mode as a per-action-chunk uncertainty signal to terminate drifting rollouts. It reports a 0.929 closed-loop Pearson correlation and 0.119 MMRV across seven real-world VLA policies, outperforms three prior video-model baselines, generalizes to new tasks, and reproduces real-world failure modes for diagnostic comparison. Authored by a heavyweight list (Levine, Quan Vuong, Ming-Yu Liu, Allen Ren, Lucy Shi and others). Read it as the evaluation layer being rebuilt on video world models, where the scarce input is the real-world rollout data the simulator has to be anchored and calibrated against. [arXiv 2606.18610](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18610), [project page](https://weichengtseng.github.io/sc3-eval/)
