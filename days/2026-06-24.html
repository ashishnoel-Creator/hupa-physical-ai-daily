    Data annotation & validation

    • ABB’s Physical AI Toolchain is the validation signal of the day, because the validation step is now a sold product, not an internal chore. Debuted publicly at Automate 2026, the toolchain covers data generation, training and validation, deployment and optimization for robot AI models, and it is the operational complement to ABB’s March NVIDIA partnership that produced RobotStudio HyperReality (NVIDIA Omniverse plus a firmware-faithful virtual controller, claimed ~99% sim-to-real, pitched as up to 80% less commissioning time). When a vendor the size of ABB packages “training and validation” as a named layer of an industrial toolchain, it is confirmation that the verify-the-data work carries enough value to sell separately. The read for us: the validation layer is being claimed by the incumbents from the simulation side, which raises the bar on what an independent capture-and-validate shop has to prove against real-world ground truth. Robotics 24/7, Tech Times
    • RobOmni is the cleaner research-grade validation story, the first omni-modal benchmark that scores manipulation with tactile in the loop. Daimon Robotics (Hong Kong) and Galbot launched RobOmni at ICRA 2026 (writeup June 8), built on NVIDIA Isaac Sim, to answer one question that has no standard answer yet: how much does tactile sensing actually improve contact-rich manipulation. It evaluates across fingertip tactile, wrist RGB, gripper state, TCP trajectories and external cameras, supports cross-embodiment comparison, and crucially runs tactile ablation studies (same model with and without touch) to isolate the contribution. It pairs with Daimon-Infinity, which Daimon released in April as the largest omni-modal robotic dataset including high-resolution tactile. Daimon frames the goal as moving physical interaction from “demo-driven” to “standard-driven,” which is the same argument the thesis makes about audited capture: the field only progresses once you can measure data quality, not just demo it. A couple of weeks old, but it never cleared this brief before and it is squarely on-thesis. Robotics 24/7, Daimon-Infinity

    Embedded AI systems

    • Kawasaki premiered the RL030N, billed as the first 8-axis robot built for Physical AI, and the load-bearing feature is the open real-time API, not the extra joints. World premiere at Automate 2026 (June 22). The two extra axes (a “diving board” elbow extension) kill the singularity dead zones that block a standard six-axis arm from confined spaces, and it holds full payload at full extension. The part that matters for the embedded stack is KRNX, an open real-time API that lets external AI software, ROS, ML systems and third-party orchestration drive the arm directly, so it slots into the physical-AI training and VLA pipelines already in use rather than running closed Kawasaki code. Hardware vendors are opening the control loop to let someone else’s model run the robot. Tech Times, Kawasaki via BusinessWire
    • R-noid runs a generalizing VLA model on a commercial robot, which is the on-device read of the Robot.com launch. Robot.com describes R-noid’s intelligence as a Vision-Language-Action model that generalizes across tasks, shipping today across kitchens, pack lines and event floors rather than as a research demo. Whether that VLA runs on the edge or leans on the cloud is the number to watch as the RaaS fleet scales, the same open question Genesis Eno’s GENE model raised last week. Robotics 24/7

    Robotics & humanoids

    • Robot.com’s R-noid is the commercial humanoid signal of the day, and it sells the deployment model, not the machine. The delivery-robot company (formerly Kiwibot) launched R-noid as a general-purpose wheeled humanoid for “the repetitive, multi-shift, hard-to-staff jobs,” five role categories (restaurant assistant, packer, picker, folder, host) across six verticals, 19 deployable tasks at launch. Hardware is dual 7-DoF arms, a 4-DoF torso with 0-1.9m vertical reach and a holonomic base. The whole pitch is Robot-as-a-Service with an 8-12 week stand-up and paying lighthouse sites already running, the co-founder’s line being “weeks, not years” and “back it up with paying customers.” Same pattern as Sanctuary and Autonomique last week, deployment-and-economics over a new demo, now with a startup pricing the body as a subscription. Robotics 24/7
    • The frame around all of this is Automate 2026, the largest edition in the show’s 50-year run, where “physical AI” is the recurring phrase and the unsolved constraint named on the floor is the sim-to-real gap. 50,000+ attendees, 1,000+ exhibitors, a first-ever NVIDIA-sponsored Humanoid Pavilion with 20+ platforms, and a Humanoid Robot Forum (June 23-24) whose central debate is how much data industrial deployment actually requires and the gap between demo performance and the 99%-plus reliability a production line needs. The show’s own framing is that the industry now has the hardware and the open question is the data and software to make it reliable. That is the thesis, stated by the incumbents on a trade-show stage. Tech Times

    Research papers worth a look

    • RobOmni: an omni-modal evaluation benchmark with tactile sensing for physical interaction (Daimon Robotics + Galbot), launched at ICRA 2026, writeup June 8. Built on NVIDIA Isaac Sim, it standardizes evaluation of contact-rich manipulation across fingertip tactile, wrist RGB, gripper state, TCP trajectories, action commands and external cameras, with a 1:1 digital twin of Daimon’s DM-TacClaw gripper and support for multiple embodiments (arms and humanoids). It scores beyond success rate (manipulation efficiency, dexterity, failure events like slip and jam, generalization) and supports tactile ablation to quantify exactly how much touch adds. Read it as evaluation infrastructure catching up to model development, the same lesson SC3-Eval taught from the video-model side: progress needs measurement, and measurement needs ground-truth data. Robotics 24/7
    • Daimon-Infinity: the largest omni-modal robotic dataset for physical AI including high-resolution tactile (Daimon Robotics), released April 2026. The dataset side of the same “Sensing - Data - Model” stack RobOmni evaluates against. Logged here for the first time because it anchors the RobOmni benchmark and is a rare large open dataset that puts tactile alongside vision. ModelScope
