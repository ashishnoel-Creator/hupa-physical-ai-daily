    TL;DR

    • NVIDIA’s GEAR lab put frontier coding agents in charge of improving real robots, and they hit a 99% success rate with the humans mostly out of the loop. This is the autoresearch loop arriving in the physical world. ENPIRE (NVIDIA, CMU, UC Berkeley) is a harness that hands Codex, Claude Code and Kimi Code a fleet of real robots plus an auto-reset and auto-verify interface, and lets them propose algorithms, run rollouts, read papers and refine until tasks like zip-tie cutting and GPU insertion reach 99% pass@8. The piece we care about is the verifier. The reward is a detector plus segmentation fusing per-camera binary verdicts, automated outcome QC standing in for human judgment. ENPIRE, NVIDIA GEAR
    • JD turned its crowd-capture program into a physical institution, and named the data vendor underneath it. On June 16 Yiren Technology, JD, China Machinery Engineering Group and Suqian High-tech Zone launched an Embodied AI Research Institute built around a “large-scale data collection” model, opening standardized capture to students, residents, factory workers and the flexible workforce, with a Suqian center that already holds nearly 10,000 concurrent operators. Yiren is described as a major China embodied-data player doing collection, annotation and simulation training. China keeps building the capture institution. Nobody local to us has one. Gasgoo, JD Suqian institute

    Data collection

    • The Suqian institute is the collection signal of the day, and the new part is that it is a building with a vendor’s name on it, not just a hiring drive. JD’s 600,000-person program and its 5M-then-10M-hour targets were already on the tape. What is new is the institutional form. Yiren Technology anchors a research institute that coordinates on-campus training production lines with distributed collection points across homes, workshops, industrial parks and logistics hubs, opening standardized capture channels to ordinary residents with lightweight equipment, a dual-track model of “community crowdsourcing” plus “fixed-point industrial collection”. In Suqian alone, 100,000+ residents are expected across 100+ scenarios. The read for us is the same one that keeps sharpening. China is making capture a civic utility with a named data company running the floor. The Indian equivalent does not exist at any layer. Gasgoo, JD Suqian institute
    • ENPIRE quietly reframes part of the collection problem as a verification problem. Its environment module auto-randomizes the starting state and scores each trial off cameras, a detector and a segmentation model, no human in the scoring loop. That is the same automated-QC machinery a capture business needs, pointed at task outcomes instead of clips. If the field standardizes on agent-run, auto-verified rollouts, the demand shifts from raw demonstration hours toward whoever can certify outcomes and data cleanly enough for an agent to trust. ENPIRE, NVIDIA GEAR

    Embedded AI systems

    • The fresh embedded-adjacent read is ENPIRE’s resource accounting, and it is a warning for anyone selling “agents will run the robot.” The paper measures Mean Robot Utilization and Mean Token Utilization and finds that as you scale the robot fleet, robot utilization falls while token consumption climbs, because the agents spend more time reading logs and summarizing each other than operating hardware. Larger fleets reach success sooner but burn far more tokens. The honest takeaway is that physical autoresearch is bottlenecked by compute and coordination, not just robots, which is the same edge-budget question the WAM cluster keeps circling. ENPIRE, NVIDIA GEAR

    Robotics & humanoids

    • ENPIRE is the capability marker of the day, and it is a different class than a backflip demo. Coding agents working only through an automated reset-and-verify interface drove dexterous tasks (Push-T, pin insertion, zip-tie tying and cutting, GPU insertion) to a 99% pass@8 success rate, with an idea-tree showing which agent-proposed changes actually moved the score. It is still a constrained-task result on a fixed rig, so weight it as a method, not a deployment. But the direction is real. The human algorithmic-engineering bottleneck is the thing being automated. ENPIRE, NVIDIA GEAR

    Research papers worth a look

    • ENPIRE: Agentic Robot Policy Self-Improvement in the Real World (NVIDIA, CMU, UC Berkeley). A harness framework with four modules (Environment for auto reset and verification, Policy Improvement, Rollout on single or parallel robots, Evolution where agents analyze logs and consult literature) that lets frontier coding agents run real-world robot research autonomously and reach 99% pass@8 on dexterous tasks. It proposes Mean Robot Utilization and Mean Token Utilization as efficiency metrics for multi-agent physical autoresearch. Two things make it worth a careful read for a data business. The automated verifier is outcome-QC without a human, and the limitations section quantifies that scaling robots drives token cost up while robot utilization falls. Read it as the autoresearch loop crossing into hardware, where the scarce inputs become a trustworthy reset-and-verify interface and clean data an agent can act on. ENPIRE, NVIDIA GEAR
