    Data collection

    • ABC-130K is the capture signal of the day, and it sets a new public floor for scale: 3,553 hours, 134,806 episodes, 195 tasks of bimanual manipulation, all open. The dataset spans pick-and-place, folding, handover, insertion, tool use and assembly, with episodes from 7 seconds to 469 seconds, and it ships with the collection method, not just the corpus. The rig is deliberately cheap: passive GELLO-style leader arms the operator moves by hand, with an encoder-only readout driving the followers through inverse kinematics, so the same hardware records demos and streams live corrections. They also introduce passive-leader DAgger, collecting intervention data when a policy is about to fail, which took a box-folding policy from 24% to 85% mean progress after two short rounds. The read for us is twofold. First, the largest open robot-data release to date is teleoperation in a lab, which is exactly the distribution that an in-the-field capture layer does not cover. Second, the part everyone will copy is the collection method, and a cheap reproducible rig plus an intervention loop is a recipe, not a moat. The moat stays in what you point it at and whether you can prove the data is clean. abc.bot, data on HuggingFace

    Data annotation & validation

    • The half of ABC nobody is quoting yet is the evaluation half, and it is a quietly strong validation result: training loss predicts real-world success, validation loss does not. Across 16 checkpoints spanning different architectures, batch sizes and compute, real strict-success correlated with training loss at r = -0.84 and with validation action error at r = -0.89, but with validation loss at r = -0.04, essentially zero. They also matched sim-eval against real-robot eval across 12 checkpoints and got r = 0.85 on strict success and r = 0.91 on task progress, strong enough to iterate in simulation before touching a robot. The team ran 100+ hours of physical evals at 50 trials per task against a fixed rubric and released the rubric and the logs. This is the measurement discipline the audited-capture argument keeps pointing at, stated from the model side: the obvious quality metric (validation loss) is the one that does not track real performance, so what you measure decides what you ship. A released rubric for grading a policy is the same object class as a released spec for grading captured data. abc.bot

    Embedded AI systems

    • DaxAI’s DaxBrain-WM is the embedded read, an embodied world model built to run on-device rather than call the cloud. The architecture is three layers: a perception layer doing millisecond-level fusion of vision, touch, force and proprioception into a real-time 3D model, a cognitive layer that predicts object motion and simulates an action’s outcome before executing it, and an action layer that converts that into dexterous motion at under 50ms latency. DaxAI pitches on-device inference, extreme cost-efficiency and fast payback, with a humanoid starting at 69,800 yuan and a line scaling from 1,000 to 10,000 units a year. Put it next to last week’s run of Chinese world-model bets (ANYVERSE, Zhicheng, Alibaba’s Qwen-RobotWorld) and the pattern sharpens: the Chinese embodied stack is converging on world models that run at the edge and are priced for volume, not cloud VLA sold per call. The differentiator under all of it is still whose environments the world model was trained and validated against. Gasgoo

    Robotics & humanoids

    • CATL and Galbot is the deployment signal, and it is a different shape from a humanoid startup selling units: the world’s largest battery maker is integrating a robot into its own production and its own service network. The Galbot S1 is already running long-range autonomous tasks inside CATL’s smart manufacturing facilities, doing material transport and component picking in battery module and pack production, with a dual-arm 50kg payload, centimeter-level vision positioning and 360-degree obstacle avoidance. The structurally new part is the second half of the deal: CATL is extending its existing battery testing, maintenance, repair and recycling network to cover humanoids, which it calls the first aftermarket service framework for embodied intelligence. Power, body and lifecycle service folded into one industrial player. That is vertical integration the Western best-of-breed stack has no direct equivalent for, and Galbot had already raised close to 5B yuan in three months going into it. Gasgoo

    Research papers worth a look

    • ABC: Scalable Behavior Cloning with Open Data, Training, and Evaluation (Allshire, H.G. Singh, R. Singh, Rashid, Choi, McAllister et al.; UC Berkeley, MIT, Amazon FAR, XDOF, CMU), 2026. A fully open-source stack for bimanual manipulation built around ABC-130K, the largest bimanual teleoperation dataset to date at 3,553 hours, 134,806 episodes and 195 tasks. It releases the cheap passive-leader-arm hardware, the abcdl distributed dataloader, ABC Sim (400 hours of sim-teleop across 20 MuJoCo scenes with a Blender re-rendering pipeline), 100+ hours of real-robot eval logs with rubrics, and two reference policies, ABC-DiT (2B diffusion transformer) and ABC-VLA (4.3B Gemma 3 backbone with a 45M action head). Two findings carry past the release: validation loss is uncorrelated with real-world success while training loss and validation action error are strongly correlated, and sim-eval predicts real-eval well enough (r = 0.85-0.91) to iterate without a robot. The clearest recent statement that in robot learning, the evaluation protocol is as load-bearing as the model. abc.bot, code, data
