### Embedded AI systems

- **HIVE is the embedded signal of the day, and the interesting part is the data model, not the chip.** The company retrofits existing industrial vehicles, wheel loaders, warehouse trucks, production-line machines, with its own sensor and compute stack so they perceive, decide and act under remote supervision. The launch example is a wheel loader clearing avalanche zones on a Norwegian mountain pass, run from a remote room instead of a cab. The commercial claim is the tell. Each deployed machine-hour feeds one reinforcement loop across the whole fleet, and HIVE says that loop pushes productive machine-hour cost down 80%. Read against the capture thesis, this is a different answer to the same data problem. Instead of paying to collect demonstrations, you instrument machines that are already working and let the operating hours become the dataset. It is brownfield data, and its moat is access to installed fleets rather than to new environments. That does not compete with validated ego capture in Indian hospitals or kitchens, it sits beside it as proof that whoever owns the running hours owns the data, whether the hours come from a human head or an industrial loader. [The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/hive-brings-in-15m-to-build-physical-ai-for-industrial-machines/)

### Robotics & humanoids

- **Tesollo is the hardware move to watch, because a robotic-hand pure-play filing for IPO is a supply-chain signal, not a product one.** The Incheon company completed a Series B and named KB Securities as its IPO underwriter, targeting a listing in the coming year. Its DG-5F-S hand uses proprietary in-house actuators, weighs under 1kg, and lands at roughly 60% of the cost of its prior hand. Hands and actuators are the expensive, hard part of a humanoid, and the read here is that an Asian component maker is driving that cost down and going public on it, while the US names keep raising on the model and the body. The humanoid cost curve is being bent in the supply chain, in Korea and China, not in the foundation-model labs. For a thesis built on Asian cost arbitrage in the data layer, the same arbitrage showing up in the hardware layer is the pattern worth logging. [The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/tesollo-initiates-ipo-process-developing-humanoid-hands/)
