### Data collection

- **Tashan Tech is running capture infrastructure, not just selling sensors, and that is the on-thesis half of its round.** This year it launched two tactile data collection centers, in Beijing's Shijingshan district and Qianjiang, Hubei, built as a universal training base compatible with robots across brands, configurations and technical routes, from dexterous hands to grippers to single and dual-arm workstations. It also released the TS-Echo data sensing finger cot, which supports body-free collection by decoupling tactile information from specific robot morphologies, so data gathered on any arm or hand can be migrated to heterogeneous bodies or actuators. The company frames tactile as "the starting point for control," requiring a closed loop across chips, sensors, data, algorithms and training paradigms. A sensor vendor with 80%+ market share concluding that the business is the data loop, and building physical collection centers to own it, is the clearest supplier-side confirmation yet that capture with a spec is where the value pools. [Gasgoo](https://autonews.gasgoo.com/articles/news/seeds-tashan-tech-raises-hundreds-of-millions-holds-80-tactile-sensor-market-share-2076686151665369088)

Read with Sunday's PNDbotics deal (robot as data-acquisition platform): BeingBeyond is assembling capture-capable hardware partners across form factors, and the entry-level capture cell is now a desktop product. ### Research papers worth a look

- **FlowDAgger: Human-in-the-Loop Adaptation of Generative Robot Policies in Latent Space (arXiv 2607.08877, July 9, listed Monday; Microsoft, UW, Berkeley: Oier Mees, Maya Cakmak, Andrey Kolobov).** The week's most consequential data-economics paper. Adapts frozen flow-matching and diffusion policies from a handful of human expert interventions by inverting each correction into the latent noise that would have produced it, then training a lightweight latent policy to steer the base model. Beats supervised fine-tuning and latent RL while preserving pretrained skills. If this holds, the marginal data a deployed robot needs is not another thousand demos, it is a graded stream of expert corrections. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08877)
- **AgenticFocus: Object-Preserving Mixed Reality Synthesis from Human FPV Video for Dexterous Humanoid Learning (arXiv 2607.08857, listed Monday; Skoltech, Tsetserukou group).** Manufactures training variation from human first-person video while preserving the manipulated object. Ego capture as seed corn for synthetic augmentation. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08857)
- **DemoBridge: A Simulation-in-the-Loop Toolkit for Single-View Human Demonstration Retargeting (arXiv 2607.09519, listed Monday; Toyota Motor Europe, KU Leuven; RSS 2026 RoboData workshop).** Sim-verified retargeting from a single camera view. Lowers the hardware floor for usable human demonstrations. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09519)
- **TactiDex: A Real-World Tactile-Guided Benchmark for Human-Like Dexterous Manipulation (arXiv 2607.09190, listed Monday; ShanghaiTech).** Tactile gets its evaluation harness. Covered in the annotation section above. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09190)
- **B-spline Policy: Accelerating Manipulation Policies via B-spline Action Representations (arXiv 2607.09648, listed Monday; MIT and UT Austin: Torralba, Yuke Zhu, Yilun Du).** Compact action representations that speed up manipulation policies. Worth a read for anyone shipping policies onto constrained edge compute. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09648)
