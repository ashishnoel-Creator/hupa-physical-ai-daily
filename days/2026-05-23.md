### Data collection

- **MobileEgo Anywhere (arXiv 2605.05945)** — 200 hours of long-form egocentric data captured on commodity smartphone sensor suites, with an open-source STERA video processing pipeline that turns raw mobile captures into VLA-training-ready format. The "smartphone is good enough" thesis is now an arXiv paper with a dataset behind it. For us this is the bear case: it argues against custom head-mount rigs for a non-trivial slice of the data SOW. Worth reading the failure modes section before our next investor conversation on rig spec. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05945)

If we are pitching capture data to US OEMs in 2027, the supply chain they will be running on is now visible. [Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10741955)

### Robotics & humanoids

- **Hyundai → 25,000 Atlas across US plants.** The number was made public at a JPMorgan Boston investor session on May 19. Metaplant Georgia first (2028), Kia Georgia 2029. 25K units = 83% of the 30K/yr Atlas production capacity Boston Dynamics is building toward 2028. Read this as: Hyundai is taking essentially all of its own subsidiary's output for the next several years, which leaves very little Atlas inventory for any third-party OEM through the decade. If you wanted Atlas in your factory, your window just closed. [Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10741955), [Boston Dynamics — Atlas](https://bostondynamics.com/blog/boston-dynamics-unveils-new-atlas-robot-to-revolutionize-industry/), [Seoul Economic Daily — value chain](https://en.sedaily.com/finance/2026/05/19/hyundai-builds-robotics-value-chain-in-us-accelerating)
- **Korean Metal Workers' Union blocks Atlas deployment.** May 22, the union said publicly that no Atlas unit enters a Hyundai factory floor without a formal labor-management agreement, and that humanoid deployment will be a key agenda item in this year's wage talks. Hyundai's US Metaplant is non-union, so the immediate effect is asymmetric, the Korean plants are blocked, the US Georgia plants are not. Still, this is the first time a humanoid order has triggered a documented union veto, and the framing the union ends up with is going to be quoted across western auto for the next 18 months. ### Research papers worth a look

- **MobileEgo Anywhere (arXiv 2605.05945).** 200hr egocentric dataset captured on commodity smartphones plus an open STERA pipeline that converts raw phone captures into VLA-ready format. Direct counter-thesis to head-mounted-rig data collection. Read in detail before the next investor / partner pitch on rig spec. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05945)
- **World Action Models: The Next Frontier in Embodied AI (arXiv 2605.12090).** New survey on WAMs as unified predictive-state-plus-action foundation models. Good map of where the field is converging. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12090)
- **Physically Native World Models, Hamiltonian perspective (arXiv 2605.00412).** Argues for physics-grounded structural priors in generative world models. The other end of the WAM design space from data-only scaling. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00412)
- **Embody4D, generalist 4D world model for embodied AI (arXiv 2605.01799).** Video-to-video world model that synthesizes arbitrary novel views from monocular input, with a compositional 4D data pipeline across 30 cross-morphology manipulators. The 4D-vs-2D world model debate now has a real generalist baseline. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01799)
