    Data collection

    • LeRobot Humanoid open release (May 21). Hugging Face shipped a complete open-source bipedal humanoid: 3D-printable parts, BOM, wiring, motor setup, runtime, identification tools, training environments. ~$2,500 in parts. The pressure case: the platform also lowers the barrier for academic groups to set up their own data-collection benches, which puts a long-tail floor on the cost of demonstration data. We should treat LeRobot Humanoid as a candidate compatibility target for our headcam capture format. HF blog

    Research papers worth a look

    • Action with Visual Primitives (arXiv 2605.22183). Published May 21. Frames manipulation as composition over a learned visual primitive set, rather than direct action prediction. Worth reading as an alternative to the dense-action-decoding path the headline VLAs (pi-0.7, Helix-02) are on. arXiv
    • Behavior Cloning for Active Perception with Low-Resolution Egocentric Vision (arXiv 2605.14106). Active-perception task where a wrist-mounted egocentric RGB camera has to reposition to center a partially visible plant before grasp. Cheap rig, cheap camera, useful failure-mode reference for low-res egocentric capture. Read the resolution-vs-success-rate plots. arXiv
    • Multimodal fusion with VLA models for robotic manipulation (Information Fusion, Vol 129, May 2026). Systematic review of 100+ VLA architectures. Finding: diffusion-based decoders + hierarchical fusion improve manipulation success across the benchmark suite. Best one-doc reference if anyone on the team needs to get current on the VLA modeling frontier. ScienceDirect
