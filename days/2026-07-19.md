### Data annotation & validation

- **BadWAM breaks the main safety story world-action models were sold on, and the fix it implies is a validation job.** The NUS team shows small visual perturbations can decouple what a WAM imagines from what it executes: their action-only attack drops task success from 96.5% to 43.1%, and their stealth variant makes the robot execute harmful actions while its predicted future still looks clean. The whole appeal of WAMs was that the action could be checked against the imagined future. If that check can be spoofed, dream-versus-action consistency auditing becomes its own QC discipline, and someone has to grade it. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15207)

### Research papers worth a look

- **RoboTTT: Context Scaling for Robot Policies (arXiv 2607.15275, submitted July 16; NVIDIA GEAR, Yunfan Jiang, Chebotar, Scott Reed, Li Fei-Fei, Yuke Zhu, Jim Fan).** Test-time training folded into VLA-style policies: fast weights updated by gradient descent during inference compress 8K timesteps of history into weight space with flat latency. Results: 87% overall improvement over single-step baselines, first full completion of a five-minute ten-stage assembly task, and 8K-context pretraining beating 1K-context by 62%. The data-business read: one-shot in-context imitation from human video means human demonstrations stop being only a training-time input and become a runtime input, a standing feed every deployed robot can consume. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15275)
- **BadWAM: When World-Action Models Dream Right but Act Wrong (arXiv 2607.15207, submitted July 16; Qi Li, Xingyi Yang, Xinchao Wang, NUS).** Covered in the validation section above: adversarial visual perturbations that decouple a WAM's imagined future from its executed action, 96.5% to 43.1% success under the action-only attack, with a stealth variant that keeps the imagination looking clean. The first systematic attack surface mapped onto the architecture the whole industry just standardized on. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15207)
