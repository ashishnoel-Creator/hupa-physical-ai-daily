### Data collection

- **General Intuition is the capture signal of the day, and it is a frontal challenge to the real-world-capture argument rather than a supporting one.** The pitch is that the cheapest, most abundant action-labeled data on earth is already sitting in gameplay clips, where every frame carries a record of the button the player pressed, so you get tightly aligned observation-action pairs for free at internet scale. The demo that earns the cheque is the transfer claim: a model pretrained on 100 hours of a Fortnite-style game, repurposed to drive a quadruped through an office, needing only eight minutes of real-world data on a street to adapt. Read it straight and it argues you do not need to go collect the world, you can learn spatial and temporal reasoning in simulation-grade game environments and fine-tune on a sliver of reality. The honest counter is in the eight minutes. The game teaches navigation and reaction, but the thing it still needed real data for is the part that does not exist in any game, which is the specific physics and mess of an actual environment. For a capture thesis the read is that gameplay pretraining shrinks how much real data you need, it does not remove the need for the real data that grounds it, and the environments that sliver has to come from are exactly the ones no game engine ships with. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/general-intuitions-2-3b-bet-that-video-games-can-train-ai-agents-for-the-real-world/), [PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/news/articles/general-intuition-is-turning-video-game-clips-into-ai-training-data-for-robots)

### Embedded AI systems

- **SENAD's Insight-World V3.0 is the embedded read, an on-device world model built for one vertical and grounded in a named academic lab.** The model sits on a proprietary five-layer architecture and is pitched as giving logistics robots a "brain" that understands physical laws well enough to clear the perception-and-decision bottlenecks that have held up industrial and warehouse deployment. It is co-developed off the Embodied AI Robot Insight series with Tsinghua University's Intelligent Perception Integrated Circuits and Systems Laboratory. Put it beside the rest of the recent Chinese world-model run and the pattern holds without restating it: edge-run world models, each one grounded in the specific environment its maker sells into, here the warehouse floor. The differentiator stays the same, which is whose logistics environments the model was trained and validated against. [Gasgoo](https://autonews.gasgoo.com/articles/news/seedsembodied-logistics-platform-sainade-raises-300-million-yuan-in-series-c-2070418165413441536)
- **General Intuition's other claim is an embedded-side one worth flagging: eight minutes of on-robot data to adapt a game-pretrained model to a new physical environment.** If that number holds up outside a demo, the interesting part is not the pretraining, it is how little real data the adaptation step needs, which would move the cost of deploying into a new environment way down. That cuts both ways for a capture layer. It lowers the data volume a deployment needs, and it raises the premium on that small, correct, environment-specific slice being clean. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/general-intuitions-2-3b-bet-that-video-games-can-train-ai-agents-for-the-real-world/)

### Research papers worth a look

- **MotionVLA: Vision-Language-Action Model for Humanoid Motion (arXiv 2606.15142), June 2026.** Generates humanoid motion from a scene image plus text. The contribution is DSFT, a dual-stream frequency tokenizer that splits motion into a base stream and a physical stream and compresses each independently with DCT truncation and BPE, instead of forcing heterogeneous motion signals through one shared codebook. It is adjacent to the core data thesis rather than on it, but worth a note because it is a fresh cs.RO VLA result aimed squarely at humanoid motion generation, the layer that sits on top of whatever demonstration data trains it. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15142)
