The interesting move is a company that raised on a home-generator (Kairos-HomeWorld, covered June 16) still anchoring its model story on real environmental collection rather than pure synthesis. ### Data annotation & validation

- **The validation read today rides inside ACE-Ego's RoboTwin 2.0 number, and it is about robustness, not labeling.** A reported 90.62% success rate under the dual-arm benchmark's domain-randomization tests is a claim about generalizing across perturbed environments, which is downstream of how clean and how varied the training data was. A model that holds up under randomization is the payoff of capture-and-validation discipline upstream, and it is the metric buyers will increasingly point at instead of raw hours. Vendor-reported, so weight it as a direction, not a verified score.
