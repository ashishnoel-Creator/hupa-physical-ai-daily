### Data collection

- **The fresh capture-side read this run is indirect and it comes from ASPIRE: the data feeding the skill library is recorded multimodal traces from simulation and real robots, not new human demonstrations.** For every perception, planning, grasping and control call, ASPIRE logs the observation, the inputs, the outputs and the visual evidence, then mines those traces to localise failures and validate repairs. That is a capture strategy where the "collection" is instrumentation of the robot's own execution, and it points at the same split the column keeps surfacing, human demonstration data on one side and self-generated execution logs on the other. It does not remove the need for grounded human data, it lowers how much of it a system burns per new skill. [ASPIRE / NVIDIA GEAR](https://research.nvidia.com/labs/gear/aspire/)

### Embedded AI systems

- **The overlooked embedded bottleneck surfaced today is thermal, and Gasgoo's automotive-thermal deep dive says it is already spilling into humanoids.** The core numbers: NVIDIA's AGX-class compute in a robot faces heat flux densities of 150 to 300 W/cm² and throttles above 85°C, and servo joints account for 60 to 80% of a humanoid's total heat dissipation. The proposed answer, from Air International's Gu Jifeng, is bionic "vascular" water jackets, multi-point liquid cooling that shares the same engineering DNA as EV intelligent-driving domain controllers. The read for a capture-and-compute layer is that on-device inference for embodied systems is not just gated by TOPS or latency determinism, it is gated by whether you can pull hundreds of watts out of a moving body, and that constraint is invisible in every spec sheet that leads with compute. [Gasgoo](https://autonews.gasgoo.com/articles/news/an-undervalued-100-billion-yuan-battlefield-awakens-2072327483499008001)

### Research papers worth a look

- **ASPIRE: Agentic Skills Discovery for Robotics (NVIDIA GEAR, June 2026).** Self-improving code-as-policy system that writes and refines robot control programs and distills validated fixes into a growing, retrievable skill library. Coding agents observe multimodal sensory traces from sim and real robots, run an evolutionary search over control programs, and localise failures by inspecting salient primitive logs. Reported result: two-arm handover from a 20% baseline to 92% through iterative debugging, with the equipped agent reaching first success in fewer tokens. Collaboration across NVIDIA, UMich, UIUC, Berkeley and CMU (Runyu Lu, Yuke Zhu, Linxi Fan, Guanzhi Wang and others). Worth reading for how it treats accumulated skills, not fresh data, as the compounding asset, and where human-grounded data still has to enter. [ASPIRE / NVIDIA GEAR](https://research.nvidia.com/labs/gear/aspire/)
- *Also surfaced, not added: OGPO (arXiv 2605.03065, May) resurfaced this run on its ICML26 acceptance, a sample-efficient off-policy method for fine-tuning generative control policies to near full task success with no expert data in the online buffer. Logged in the X section as a fresh discussion, not as a new paper, since it predates the window and is already a month old. ACE-Ego-0 (arXiv 2606.17200) is already in the Papers DB.*
