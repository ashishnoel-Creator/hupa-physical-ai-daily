    Data collection

    • Xiaomi-Robotics-1 is the data story of the window dressed as a model release. The paper (arXiv 2607.15330, same submission batch as RoboTTT) describes a generalist VLA trained on 100K+ hours of real-world trajectories gathered with UMI handheld devices across a very wide environment and task spread, claiming out-of-the-box mobile manipulation in homes it has never seen. Two reads. First, the biggest disclosed real-robot corpus on record was collected with cheap portable grippers and large human data ops, which is direct validation that capture scale is an operations problem, not a hardware problem. Second, the company that published factory takt-time numbers last week is also quietly running one of the largest human-demonstration collection operations in the world. Nothing in the paper says what fraction of collected hours survived curation. That number stays the industry’s best kept secret. arXiv, Hugging Face
    • BrainCo globally unveiled a Dexterous Manipulation Data Matrix at WAIC alongside its thought-controlled robot demos. The Hangzhou BCI company is packaging dexterous-hand demonstration data as a standalone product line. A brain-interface vendor deciding the sellable asset is manipulation data, not the interface, says where the money is pointing. Gasgoo, TechNode

    Data annotation & validation

    • Never covered here until now: Mercor’s step-up round. Watch for the close. Forbes
    • The annotation research this window is about removing annotation, and that direction is the signal. AutoSpeed (arXiv 2607.01051) learns manipulation motion speed with zero speed or stage labels, and Simple-to-Complex (arXiv 2607.04591) curates VLA training data by decomposing demonstrations into skills and standardizing the environment instead of hand-labeling each clip. PRISM (arXiv 2607.04880) goes further and synthesizes personalized robot datasets from images. All three push the labeling that used to be manual into the pipeline, which is the capture-time-annotation shape we build toward. The counter-read for a validation business is that auto-curated data still needs someone to grade the curator. arXiv 2607.01051, arXiv 2607.04591, arXiv 2607.04880

    Research papers worth a look

    • Xiaomi-Robotics-1: Scaling Vision-Language-Action Models with over 100K Hours of Real-World Trajectories (arXiv 2607.15330, Xiaomi Robotics). Full read in the data collection section above. The largest disclosed real-world manipulation corpus, collected on UMI handheld hardware, trending at the top of Hugging Face papers this week. arXiv
    • From Perception to Assistance: Open-Vocabulary Shared Autonomy for Robotic Manipulation (arXiv 2607.17323, submitted July 19; USP São Carlos and Tohoku). Wearable-free teleoperation: a single RGB-D camera tracks the operator’s arm and hand, a VLM grounds the target object from a text prompt, and GPU MPC executes with collision avoidance. No exoskeleton, no calibration. The cost-per-demonstrator curve for teleop capture keeps falling, which is the curve every capture operation lives on. arXiv
    • AC-VLA: Robust Out-of-Distribution Action Execution via Compositional Learning (arXiv 2607.15714, Shenzhen Technology University). Compositional learning to keep VLA action execution stable out of distribution. OOD failure taxonomy is exactly the thing validation pipelines get paid to label, so methods papers that formalize it feed the QC spec directly. arXiv

    The estimation layer, the papers that improve the actual labels we produce (depth, pose, segmentation), is where the quiet work was this window. It rarely gets press so it never surfaces on a search-only sweep, and it is the layer that matters most to a data company.

    • FoundationGeo: Learning Spatial Pixel-Wise Fields for Monocular Metric Geometry (arXiv 2607.11588, submitted July 13; HKU and DiDi Voyager Research). Two-stage monocular metric depth: a DINOv3-initialized affine-invariant geometry model trained on 10.2M curated samples, then lightweight pixel-wise scale and ray-direction calibration fields to recover metric scale. Metric depth is one of the labels we ship, and a foundation-grade estimator sets the accuracy bar an annotation pipeline has to clear. arXiv
    • ZipDepth: Lightweight Zero-Shot Monocular Depth Anywhere, on Any Device (arXiv 2607.08771, University of Bologna). A 6.1M-parameter reparameterizable encoder-decoder distilled from a depth foundation model, running real-time on edge and roughly 50x smaller than the foundation baselines. Edge-bound depth estimation at capture time is exactly the later-generation rig spec, depth computed on the head puck instead of labeled after the fact. arXiv
    • VocaDet: Sample-Driven Open-Vocabulary Object Detection and Segmentation via Visual Tokenization and Vector Database Retrieval (arXiv 2607.08541). Open-vocabulary detect-and-segment through visual tokens plus vector-DB retrieval, so new object classes get added by example with no retrain. Auto-labeling that scales to a new object taxonomy without a training cycle is the difference between a fixed label schema and one that grows with the buyer’s spec. arXiv
    • VOCA: Visual Odometry with Codec Awareness (arXiv 2607.00189, submitted July 1). Camera-pose and visual odometry made robust to lossy video compression artifacts. Ego capture is almost always compressed before it reaches the pipeline, so head and camera-pose estimation that survives codec loss is a direct capture-spec concern, not an academic one. arXiv

    The gap worth naming honestly: no new hand-pose or body-pose estimator printed in this exact window beyond EgoHTR (covered July 20).
