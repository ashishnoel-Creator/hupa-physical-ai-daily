### Embedded AI systems

- **Intrinsic's Intelligence Cell is the embedded read of the day, because it moves the intelligence into the workcell and out of hand-written code.** Google's Intrinsic (folded into Google as of February) debuted an AI-powered cell that figures out tasks and runs factory automation without the thousands of lines of robot code its Flowstate platform used to require. The framing matters: it landed the same week QNX published research naming software, not hardware, as the biggest bottleneck to physical AI innovation. The race to make a robot useful is shifting from the body to the software layer that programs it. [The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/how-intrinsic-eliminates-manual-robot-coding/), [QNX research](https://www.therobotreport.com/software-becoming-biggest-bottleneck-physical-ai-innovation-finds-qnx/)
- **NVIDIA shipped Halos, a full-stack safety system that fuses AI compute, system software and sensor data, with Agility Robotics as first user.** Halos ties compute, software, sensor data, safety apps and inspection into one stack for robots working near people, and Agility is the launch deployment across Amazon, GXO, Schaeffler and Toyota lines. A related FORT and NVIDIA "Outside-In Safety" blueprint uses external sensors to watch autonomous robots from the environment rather than only onboard. Both are the same bet: safety for human-adjacent robots is becoming its own integrated compute-and-sensor layer. [The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/nvidia-releases-halos-a-full-stack-safety-system-for-robotics/)

### Robotics & humanoids

- **Bear-Kinisi is the consolidation signal, a service-robot company buying a wheeled humanoid and its manipulation IP to go end-to-end.** Bear, known for hospitality and logistics AMRs, is absorbing the KR1 wheeled humanoid and Kinisi's Bristol team to add physical manipulation it did not have, with the stated goal of deploying beyond its current hospitality and logistics base. The pattern to watch is platform companies buying manipulation startups to complete a stack rather than every humanoid lab staying independent. Terms were not disclosed. [The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/bear-robotics-acquires-kinisi-robotics-to-boost-its-physical-ai-capabilities/)
- **Collaborative Robotics shipped Proxie Gen 2, and the new capability is autotasking, the robot deciding what to move without being told.** CEO Brad Porter says Proxie Gen 2 can sense when materials are ready, identify where they go, and create the tasks itself, plus add mobile manipulation. That is autonomy moving up from path-following to task-generation on a commercial mobile robot, the soft version of what humanoids are chasing. [The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/cobots-proxie-gen-2-robot-adds-autotasking-mobile-manipulation/)
