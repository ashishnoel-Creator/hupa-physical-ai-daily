    Data collection

    • GRAIL is the collection-side signal today, and it reads the way every NVIDIA data-generation release this spring has read. Instead of reconstructing messy in-the-wild video, it fixes the 3D configuration first, object geometry, camera parameters, metric scale, environment depth and a robot-proportioned character all known up front, then generates the interaction video and reconstructs a clean 4D human-object trajectory from it. The output is picking, manipulation, sitting, navigating stairs, retargeted to the Unitree G1 and verified in the real world. The capture-side read is the one we keep making. As synthetic pipelines absorb more of the volume, the part that does not commoditize is the validated real anchor (the known 3D structure, the in-distribution scene, the verified-on-hardware trajectory). GRAIL needs all three to function. That is the input worth owning, not undifferentiated hours. arXiv 2606.05160, NVIDIA dataset on Hugging Face

    Research papers worth a look

    • GRAIL: Generating Humanoid Loco-Manipulation from 3D Assets and Video Priors (arXiv 2606.05160), NVIDIA Labs. A digital data-generation pipeline that composes 3D assets, simulator-ready scenes and video-foundation-model priors to synthesize humanoid loco-manipulation without rebuilding physical setups or teleoperating the robot. The design choice that matters is that it fixes the full 3D configuration (geometry, camera, metric scale, depth, a robot-proportioned character) before generating any video, then reconstructs a clean 4D human-object trajectory, retargets to the Unitree G1, and verifies on real hardware. Public dataset on Hugging Face. Read it as the strongest current statement that synthetic data scales only on top of known structure and real priors, and still has to land on a real robot. The validated anchor is the scarce input. arXiv 2606.05160
    • AffordanceVLA: A Vision-Language-Action Model Empowering Action Generation through Affordance-Aware Understanding (arXiv 2606.06155). Introduces structured affordance forecasting as a task-oriented intermediate between vision, language and action, split into Which2Act (object-centric grounding), Where2Act (2D interaction localization) and How2Act (3D geometric reasoning), inside a Mixture-of-Transformer backbone built on the open π0 / PaliGemma stack, with a data pipeline using RexOmni, Qwen3-VL, SAM and SAM-3D. Worth tracking because the affordance layer it forecasts (where on an object you can act, in 3D) is exactly the kind of label that capture-time annotation could supply against a spec. arXiv 2606.06155
    • MotionDisco: Motion Discovery for Extreme Humanoid Loco-Manipulation (arXiv 2606.06139), Taouil et al. Tackles the harder end of whole-body humanoid motion, discovering motions for extreme loco-manipulation rather than relying on a fixed demonstration set. Narrower than GRAIL, but it lands in the same week and points at the same problem the field is circling, that whole-body humanoid behavior needs motion sources that scale beyond hand-collected demos. arXiv 2606.06139
