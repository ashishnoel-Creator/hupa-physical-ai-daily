### Data annotation & validation

- **ABB's Physical AI Toolchain is the validation signal of the day, because the validation step is now a sold product, not an internal chore.** Debuted publicly at Automate 2026, the toolchain covers data generation, training and validation, deployment and optimization for robot AI models, and it is the operational complement to ABB's March NVIDIA partnership that produced RobotStudio HyperReality (NVIDIA Omniverse plus a firmware-faithful virtual controller, claimed ~99% sim-to-real, pitched as up to 80% less commissioning time). When a vendor the size of ABB packages "training and validation" as a named layer of an industrial toolchain, it is confirmation that the verify-the-data work carries enough value to sell separately. The read for us: the validation layer is being claimed by the incumbents from the simulation side, which raises the bar on what an independent capture-and-validate shop has to prove against real-world ground truth. [Robotics 24/7](https://www.robotics247.com/article/automate-2026-abb-robotics-delivers-new-industry-ready-physical-ai), [Tech Times](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318820/20260622/automate-2026-day-day-kawasaki-8-dof-premiere-abb-physical-ai-launch-humanoid-forum.htm)
- **RobOmni is the cleaner research-grade validation story, the first omni-modal benchmark that scores manipulation with tactile in the loop.** Daimon Robotics (Hong Kong) and Galbot launched RobOmni at ICRA 2026 (writeup June 8), built on NVIDIA Isaac Sim, to answer one question that has no standard answer yet: how much does tactile sensing actually improve contact-rich manipulation. It evaluates across fingertip tactile, wrist RGB, gripper state, TCP trajectories and external cameras, supports cross-embodiment comparison, and crucially runs tactile ablation studies (same model with and without touch) to isolate the contribution. It pairs with Daimon-Infinity, which Daimon released in April as the largest omni-modal robotic dataset including high-resolution tactile. Daimon frames the goal as moving physical interaction from "demo-driven" to "standard-driven," which is the same argument the thesis makes about audited capture: the field only progresses once you can measure data quality, not just demo it. A couple of weeks old, but it never cleared this brief before and it is squarely on-thesis. [Robotics 24/7](https://www.robotics247.com/article/daimon-robotics-galbot-launch-robomni-to-see-how-tactile-sensing-improves-robot-manipulation), [Daimon-Infinity](https://modelscope.cn/datasets/daimonrobotics/Daimon-Infinity)

### Embedded AI systems

- **Kawasaki premiered the RL030N, billed as the first 8-axis robot built for Physical AI, and the load-bearing feature is the open real-time API, not the extra joints.** World premiere at Automate 2026 (June 22). The two extra axes (a "diving board" elbow extension) kill the singularity dead zones that block a standard six-axis arm from confined spaces, and it holds full payload at full extension. The part that matters for the embedded stack is KRNX, an open real-time API that lets external AI software, ROS, ML systems and third-party orchestration drive the arm directly, so it slots into the physical-AI training and VLA pipelines already in use rather than running closed Kawasaki code. Hardware vendors are opening the control loop to let someone else's model run the robot. [Tech Times](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318820/20260622/automate-2026-day-day-kawasaki-8-dof-premiere-abb-physical-ai-launch-humanoid-forum.htm), [Kawasaki via BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260616084721/en/Kawasaki-Robotics-Unveils-Dexterous-Physical-AI-Robot-Platform-Advanced-Automation-Technologies-at-Automate-2026)

### Research papers worth a look

- **RobOmni: an omni-modal evaluation benchmark with tactile sensing for physical interaction (Daimon Robotics + Galbot), launched at ICRA 2026, writeup June 8.** Built on NVIDIA Isaac Sim, it standardizes evaluation of contact-rich manipulation across fingertip tactile, wrist RGB, gripper state, TCP trajectories, action commands and external cameras, with a 1:1 digital twin of Daimon's DM-TacClaw gripper and support for multiple embodiments (arms and humanoids). It scores beyond success rate (manipulation efficiency, dexterity, failure events like slip and jam, generalization) and supports tactile ablation to quantify exactly how much touch adds. Read it as evaluation infrastructure catching up to model development, the same lesson SC3-Eval taught from the video-model side: progress needs measurement, and measurement needs ground-truth data. [Robotics 24/7](https://www.robotics247.com/article/daimon-robotics-galbot-launch-robomni-to-see-how-tactile-sensing-improves-robot-manipulation)
- **Daimon-Infinity: the largest omni-modal robotic dataset for physical AI including high-resolution tactile (Daimon Robotics), released April 2026.** The dataset side of the same "Sensing - Data - Model" stack RobOmni evaluates against. Logged here for the first time because it anchors the RobOmni benchmark and is a rare large open dataset that puts tactile alongside vision. [ModelScope](https://modelscope.cn/datasets/daimonrobotics/Daimon-Infinity)
