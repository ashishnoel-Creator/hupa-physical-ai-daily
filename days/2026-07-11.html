CAICT released the Industrial Embodied AI Robot Training Dataset 2.0 on July 8, built on “work-as-acquisition”: head-mounted, egocentric, full-body capture rigs from Deta Intelligence worn by frontline workers at real automotive workstations, collecting during actual production with no interruption, plus the industry’s first trial-and-error data system that records deviation, correction and completion. Tsinghua, Peking University, JD.com, ZTE, Estun and Hisense are co-builders. Gasgoo

    Data collection

    • The CAICT dataset is the most on-thesis document this column has logged in weeks, because it codifies validated egocentric capture as national industrial practice. Three design choices stand out. First, “work-as-acquisition”: Deta Intelligence’s head-mounted full-panoramic rig sits on workers doing their normal shift, so collection needs no staged environment and does not stop the line, and it records hands, torso, legs and feet against the environment simultaneously. Second, the trial-and-error system captures the full arc from deviation to identification to adjustment to completion, which is exactly the error-recovery data every imitation pipeline is missing. Third, “real-scene data refining” embeds collection teams inside automotive factories so data is sourced, trained and applied in the same industrial field. The uncomfortable read from Bengaluru: the capture-at-work model HuPa runs in Indian hospitals and F&B spaces is now standardized, state-endorsed practice in China, with a components partner (Deta) already at large-scale rig deployment. The environments still do not transfer. The operating model clearly does. Gasgoo
    • General Intuition is the counter-position, stated more bluntly than anyone has said it on the record. Pim de Witte’s claim is that a foundation model trained on millions of hours of video game footage plus controller-action streams already carries the spatial-temporal intuition, so real-world data shrinks to a fine-tuning garnish: eight minutes of robot data to run a quadruped zero-shot on one camera, with dynamic obstacles. Vinod Khosla is backing the thesis with $320M at $2.3B. Two honest reads. If transfer from game worlds holds for manipulation the way it held for locomotion, generic real-world capture is in trouble. But locomotion is the easy end, the model still needed those eight real minutes, and nothing in a game engine contains the contact physics of a wet hospital tray or a tandoor station. The claim to watch is whether “a few minutes” survives contact-rich dexterity. TechCrunch

    Data annotation & validation

    • Amazon is closing Mechanical Turk to new customers on July 30, ending two decades of the original crowd-labeling marketplace as an open service. Existing customers keep access but AWS says no new features are coming. The two details worth keeping: a 2023 study estimated 33-46% of MTurk workers were already using LLMs to do their “human” labeling, and Amazon’s investment moved to SageMaker Ground Truth years ago. The commodity end of annotation did not lose to cheaper humans, it lost to models pretending to be humans, which is precisely the failure mode a validated pipeline with an audit trail exists to rule out. Entry-tier microtask labeling is now formally a dead market. Provenance-graded work is what remains. TechCrunch, The Register
    • RoboDojo landed as a unified sim-and-real benchmark for evaluating generalist manipulation policies, with 133 upvote-traction on the Hub. Evaluation infrastructure is the validation layer’s public face, and a shared sim-plus-real harness makes “how good is this policy” a reproducible claim instead of a demo reel. The same standardization pressure that hit capture tooling is now hitting evaluation. arXiv
    • The named vendors (Scale, Mercor, Surge, Labelbox, Snorkel, Sama, iMerit, Invisible, Centific, Toloka) published nothing dated this window.

    Embedded AI systems

    • LingBot-World 2.0 shipped with a 1.3B distilled variant that runs the full interactive world model on a single GPU, and a real-time variant driving 720p at 60 fps. The headline features are the unbounded interaction horizon and the agentic harness (a pilot agent executes behaviors while a director agent synthesizes the scene), but the embedded signal is the deployment math: a world model compact enough to sit next to the policy rather than in a datacenter. Ant’s Robbyant group paired the 14B flagship with the small model deliberately. arXiv
    • LingBot-Video, the same group’s open MoE video foundation model for embodied intelligence, makes inference efficiency a first-class design constraint. The stated reason for Mixture-of-Experts over dense is capacity without paying for it at inference, the same trade LingBot-VA 2.0 made two days earlier. Every frontier embodied release out of this group now optimizes for the run-time budget of a robot, not the render farm of a studio. arXiv

    Robotics & humanoids

    • Humanoid (the London one) published KinetIQ Ascend, a real-world RL method it claims scales to 99.9% manipulation reliability, with numbers on production-shaped tasks. Machine feeding: throughput up 42%, running at 1.5x the speed of the human demonstrations it learned from. Cluttered tote picking and handover: throughput up 85%, success 80% to 98%. Bimanual tote lifting: throughput doubled, success 78% to 99%, a ~20x failure reduction, all after days of training rather than months of tuning. CTO Jarad Cannon’s framing, robots “outperforming human demonstrations within days,” is the demonstrations-as-floor-not-ceiling argument made commercial. The catch the column keeps flagging: RL refines a behavior it already has, and the seed behavior still comes from human demonstration data. The report even documents arm drift after long RL runs, which is what unsupervised self-improvement looks like when nobody is grading it. The Robot Report, technical report
    • Fourier and GigaAI signed a strategic partnership on July 10 around a “body + model + data platform” stack. Fourier brings the GRx humanoids, toolchains and its 2,000+ institution rehab footprint across 40 countries; GigaAI brings its GigaWorld world model and GigaBrain VLA/WA stack, claiming 10-100x efficiency gains across the data, training and testing chain. Another Chinese pairing where the body maker and the world-model shop integrate rather than compete, aimed at healthcare and manufacturing rollouts. Gasgoo

    Research papers worth a look

    • LingBot-Video: Scaling Mixture-of-Experts Video Pretraining for Embodied Intelligence (arXiv 2607.07675, July 8). the open MoE video foundation model, #1 HF paper of the day. Built from scratch for embodiment rather than adapted from content generation: MoE for inference efficiency, a data profiling engine that augments internet video with manipulation, navigation and egocentric footage, and a multi-dimensional reward system enforcing physical rationality and task completion over aesthetics. Code out. The “egocentric footage as required diet” detail is the demand signal. arXiv
    • LingBot-World 2.0 / Infinite Worlds with Versatile Interactions (arXiv 2607.07534, July 8). real-time interactive world model, 720p at 60 fps. Unbounded interaction horizon via causal pretraining, a distilled real-time variant, an agentic pilot-plus-director harness, multiplayer, and a 1.3B model that runs on one GPU. The world-model-as-data-engine bet, now fast enough to sit in a control loop. arXiv
    • LaMem-VLA: Dual Latent Memory in Vision-Language-Action Models (arXiv 2607.07608, July 8). Attacks the Markovian blind spot in mainstream VLAs by reconstructing history into latent memory tokens, short-term and long-term vaults with a curator, seeker, condenser and weaver, all in the model’s native embedding space. Long-horizon, temporally dependent tasks are where demonstration data with real task arcs beats clip soup. arXiv
    • RoboDojo: A Unified Sim-and-Real Benchmark for Generalist Robot Manipulation Policies (arXiv 2607.04434, early July). Shared evaluation harness across simulation and real hardware for generalist policies. Evaluation standardizing is the validation layer going public. arXiv
    • OmniTacTune: Policy-Agnostic Real-World RL for Tactile Residual Adaptation of Visual Policies (arXiv 2607.03723, early July, UMD). Bolts tactile residual adaptation onto any visual policy via real-world RL. Tactile keeps being the modality that fixes what vision-only capture misses. arXiv
    • WildCity: A Real-World City-Scale Testbed for Rendering, Simulation, and Spatial Intelligence (arXiv 2607.06838, July, NVIDIA). City-scale real-world testbed for simulation and spatial intelligence. The sim stack keeps annexing bigger chunks of the real world as reference data. arXiv
    • CAICT Industrial Embodied AI Robot Training Dataset 2.0 (released July 8). the state-built industrial ego dataset. Trial-and-error data system, work-as-acquisition egocentric capture via Deta Intelligence rigs, collection embedded in real automotive production. Tracked here as a dataset release; no public download link yet in the English coverage. Gasgoo
    • KinetIQ Ascend technical report (Humanoid, July). Full methodology for the real-world RL results above: training infrastructure, algorithms, the arm-drift failure analysis. Worth reading for the honest failure section as much as the throughput claims. Humanoid
