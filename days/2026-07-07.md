### Data collection

- **QUANXTA Zero is the capture signal of the day, and it is squarely on the wedge.** X Square frames the problem exactly the way this column does: robotics data is harder to get than language data, and embodied AI is stuck on three bottlenecks, limited volume, low collection efficiency and inconsistent quality. Its answer is a three-product matrix built on universal manipulation interfaces. Zero-G1 (UMI-VIO) is the flagship, a single headband plus dual grippers capturing visual, tactile and audio in one system, with multi-view coverage that pairs first-person perception with close-range wrist views to get grasp, contact and occlusion detail. Zero-G0 (UMI-VR) is a PICO 4 headset plus dual-gripper backpack rig for whole-body mobile capture. Zero-E0 (Ego) is a lightweight six-camera first-person context device. The efficiency claim is the number to hold: nearly 100 demonstrations per hour, 2.33x conventional teleoperation, with one-click start-stop and a magnetic hot-swappable battery for all-day operation. The "embodiment-free" framing matters. This is human capture through a gripper interface that replays across different robot bodies, which is the same bet as TrainThemAI and X Square's own XRZero-G0, now packaged as a full product line rather than an open dataset. [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/x-square-robot-launches-quanxta-zero-series-reframing-embodied-data-production-through-universal-manipulation-interfaces-302818902.html)

### Data annotation & validation

- **The validation layer is where QUANXTA is most interesting, because X Square built the whole thing in-house and put a yield number on it.** The pipeline runs four stages. High-fidelity multi-source sync uses high-frequency temporal alignment and interpolation-based cleaning to hit microsecond timestamp alignment. Automated cleaning and intelligent annotation removes low-value segments (pauses, prep motions, failures, abnormal trajectories) and uses multimodal foundation models for action segmentation and semantic labeling. Human-AI QC routes by AI confidence, high-confidence data flows automatically and only low-confidence samples go to human review, and X Square claims this raises data yield to up to 85%. The fourth stage closes the loop into training and evaluation, then identifies weak scenarios to supplement with targeted new capture. Two reads for the thesis. First, 85% yield through a self-run confidence-routing loop is a strong number, and it is the buyer answering the validation question themselves rather than buying an external audit. Second, and the part worth staring at, QUANXTA ships face and background blurring, dynamic watermarking and short-term access controls in the platform. That is the data-governance and provenance layer, the thing that lets a buyer trust where data came from and who touched it, now bundled into a capture product from a robot lab. When the audit trail is in the box, "we validate your data" stops being a standalone pitch.
