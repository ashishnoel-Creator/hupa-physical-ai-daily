    TL;DR

    • A Chinese lab open-sourced a VLA model that it claims tops NVIDIA’s GR00T and Physical Intelligence’s π₀.₅ on a humanoid benchmark, and the part that matters is what sits under it: a data-collection-first paradigm. The model is built on ACE’s “environmental data collection to Kaiwu World Model 3.0 to embodied interaction” stack, the same company whose home-generator and Angel+ round we covered two days ago. China keeps wiring the silicon layer under the bodies, on domestic parts. Gasgoo, Leju x Huaxi

    Data collection

    • ACE-Ego is the collection signal today even though it ships as a model, because ACE keeps putting the capture layer first in its own framing. The release describes ACE-Ego as the output of a “Human-centric” research paradigm introduced in December 2025 that runs “environmental data collection to Kaiwu World Model 3.0 to embodied interaction”, treating the laws of human-physical-world interaction as the starting point. The interesting move is a company that raised on a home-generator (Kairos-HomeWorld, covered June 16) still anchoring its model story on real environmental collection rather than pure synthesis. Generated homes and a captured-data paradigm are being run side by side, not one instead of the other, which is the same conclusion the synthesis-versus-capture tension keeps reaching. The retail tasks it now handles (packing plastic bags, boxing shoes, product sorting, order fulfillment) are exactly the strong-contact, long-cycle work that needs validated real demonstration data to train. Gasgoo, ACE-Ego

    Data annotation & validation

    • The validation read today rides inside ACE-Ego’s RoboTwin 2.0 number, and it is about robustness, not labeling. A reported 90.62% success rate under the dual-arm benchmark’s domain-randomization tests is a claim about generalizing across perturbed environments, which is downstream of how clean and how varied the training data was. A model that holds up under randomization is the payoff of capture-and-validation discipline upstream, and it is the metric buyers will increasingly point at instead of raw hours. Vendor-reported, so weight it as a direction, not a verified score. Gasgoo, ACE-Ego
    • No new public move from the named Western annotation vendors (Scale, Mercor, Surge, Labelbox, Snorkel, Sama, iMerit, Invisible, Centific, Toloka) surfaced in the window.

    Embedded AI systems

    • Leju and Huaxi is the embedded signal of the day, and the framing is the whole point: a “data-model-chip” full chain on domestic silicon. The two will co-develop domestic high-compute main-control solutions for edge perception, inference and control, push embodied large models and motion-control models to run efficiently on the edge, and build a developer ecosystem with SDKs and open-source projects. Huaxi’s RISE computing platform runs its in-house R1 chip, in mass production since October 2025, already powering the Zhiyuan Genie G2 and integrated into Jiushi’s L4 logistics vehicles. This is the same domestic-chip-stack thread as D-Robotics RDK, now with a second named platform pairing directly with a top-five body maker. The edge layer in China is consolidating on local parts while the West still defaults to Jetson. Gasgoo, Leju x Huaxi
    • A second domestic-compute datapoint landed the same day: construction started on what Gasgoo calls Hong Kong’s largest domestic intelligent computing center, tied to SenseTime. It is infrastructure, not a robot, but it is the same direction as the chip pact above. The compute under Chinese physical AI is being built on Chinese silicon and Chinese data centers as a deliberate stack. Gasgoo, HK computing center
