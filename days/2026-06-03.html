    Data collection

    • CVPR opening is the collection-side event of the week even though no new commercial rig dropped overnight. The egocentric world-model cluster is the signal. Two papers in the CVPR window matter for anyone selling captured ego data. “Walk through Paintings: Egocentric World Models from Internet Priors” builds an action-conditioned egocentric world model out of internet-scale video priors, and EgoLive constructs robot-aligned training data directly from large-scale real-world human egocentric video. Read together they push the same way the field has pushed all spring. Internet priors plus a smaller validated real corpus is starting to beat raw hour count. That cuts toward the HuPa wedge, not against it. What survives when synthetic and internet priors do the heavy lifting is coverage no public corpus has and validated yield against a spec, not undifferentiated hours.     Research papers worth a look

    • Walk through Paintings: Egocentric World Models from Internet Priors (arXiv 2601.15284), CVPR 2026. Turns internet-scale video priors into an action-conditioned egocentric world model, so a model can roll out plausible first-person futures in scenes it never trained on. The capture-side read is the one this brief keeps making. Each step toward usable world models from internet and synthetic priors re-prices raw captured hours, and what is left defensible is the validated, in-distribution, hard-to-source corpus, not volume. arXiv 2601.15284
    • EgoLive: A Large-Scale Egocentric Dataset from Real-World Human Tasks (arXiv 2604.23570). Extends egocentric pre-training by building robot-aligned training data out of human egocentric video of real tasks, pushing the human-video-to-robot-policy transfer line that EgoScale, In-N-On and EgoVerse all sit on. The relevant point for a workplace-capture wedge is that the field is converging on real human task video as the substrate, which is exactly what rigs in hospitals, F&B and factories produce, as long as it is captured against a spec and validated. arXiv 2604.23570
