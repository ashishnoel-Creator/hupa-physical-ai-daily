### Data collection

- **PointWorld is the collection-side story today even though it is a world-model paper, because of what it implies about data.** The training set is ~2M trajectories / 500 hours split across one single-arm Franka and one bimanual humanoid, and the model generalizes to in-the-wild scenes from a single image. That is a very small, very concentrated dataset producing broad zero-shot manipulation. The read for any captured-data wedge: scale of hours is not the moat if a 500-hour, two-embodiment corpus plus a 3D-point-flow representation gets you in-the-wild tool use with no demos. What survives as defensible is distribution coverage (environments and hands not in any public corpus) and validated, audit-trailed yield, not the raw hour count. [arXiv 2601.03782](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03782), [project page](https://point-world.github.io/)

### Research papers worth a look

- **PointWorld: Scaling 3D World Models for In-The-Wild Robotic Manipulation (arXiv 2601.03782, CVPR 2026 highlight).** A pre-trained 3D world model that unifies state and action as 3D point flows in a shared 3D space. Given one or few RGB-D images plus low-level robot action commands, it forecasts per-pixel 3D displacements that respond to those actions, conditioning directly on robot geometry and integrating across embodiments. Trained on ~2M trajectories / 500 hours (single-arm Franka + bimanual humanoid), real-time 0.1s inference for MPC, single checkpoint doing pushing, deformable / articulated manipulation and tool use zero-shot from a single in-the-wild image with no demos or post-training. The most important read of the weekend for anyone whose pitch rests on raw captured hours. [arXiv 2601.03782](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03782), [project page](https://point-world.github.io/)
- **Vision-Language-Action in Robotics: A Survey of Datasets, Benchmarks, and Data Engines (arXiv 2604.23001).** Comprehensive survey of the VLA stack from the data side, covering datasets, benchmarks and the data engines that feed them. Useful as a single reference map of which corpora and collection pipelines the field actually trains on, and where the gaps are. Worth a skim before any buyside conversation that needs the lay of the data land in one document. [arXiv 2604.23001](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.23001v1)
