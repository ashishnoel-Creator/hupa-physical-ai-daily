    TL;DR

    • NVIDIA’s first open humanoid reference robot is a Chinese chassis. The Isaac GR00T Reference Humanoid Robot announced at GTC Taipei yesterday is Unitree’s H2 Plus, and it ships to Stanford, ETH Zurich, UC San Diego and Ai2 in October. The build is a Unitree H2 (6ft, 150lb, 31 DOF) plus dual Sharpa Wave five-finger tactile hands (22 DOF, 75 DOF total) running Jetson Thor and the open Isaac GR00T stack. Anyone can buy it. This is the embodiment story of the quarter. The reference robot every Western academic VLA paper trains on for the next year is now a single fixed platform, and it is Chinese hardware running US silicon. NVIDIA newsroom, Unitree PR, CNBC
    • Unitree cleared its STAR Market listing review on June 1. China now has its first listed pure-play humanoid robotics company. The Shanghai Stock Exchange listing committee passed the application, ¥4.202B ($619M) target raise at a ~¥42B ($6.2B) valuation, 73 days from formal acceptance on March 20. The clearance and the NVIDIA reference-robot reveal landed the same morning, which is not a coincidence. Every Chinese humanoid valuation in 2026 now reprices against a liquid public benchmark. Seoul Economic Daily, Caixin, Pandaily
    • The US-stack-vs-China-stack framing this brief has carried for two weeks just collapsed into one hybrid stack at the research layer. US lawmakers have a live bill that would ban federally-funded researchers from using Unitree robots over alleged government and military ties, and NVIDIA’s answer is to route every software update for the robot’s subsystems through its own chip for authenticity checking. So the reference humanoid for Stanford and UCSD is Chinese, the compute is American, and the security model is a US chip policing Chinese firmware. The clean bifurcation story is dead. For HuPa the read sharpens. When the academic embodiment standardizes on one chassis, the contested layer is the data that trains it, and the distribution gap (Indian hands and environments missing from every public corpus) does not care which flag the chassis flies. CNBC, 933 The Drive

    Data collection

    • A standardized reference embodiment is a quiet repricing of captured-demonstration data, and it cuts toward the HuPa wedge. Until yesterday every lab collecting human demonstrations had to reason about which robot the data would map onto. With H2 Plus shipping as the common research platform to Ai2, ETH, Stanford and UCSD in October, demonstration data collected anywhere now has a single dominant academic target to retarget against. That lowers cross-embodiment friction, which is exactly the friction that used to make raw hours feel defensible. What survives the standardization is coverage and yield. Environments, hands and demographics that are not in any public corpus, captured against a published spec with an audit trail, not hour count on a generic rig. NVIDIA newsroom

    Embedded AI systems

    • Jetson Thor shipping specs are now firm, not previewed. 2,070 FP4 TFLOPs, 7.5x the compute of Jetson Orin, 3.5x better energy efficiency, 40-130W configurable. This is the onboard inference engine inside H2 Plus, so the reference research humanoid and the reference edge compute ship as one bundle in October. The same Blackwell-at-the-edge architecture scales up into the N1X laptop SoC that NVIDIA formally announced (20-core ARM CPU, integrated Blackwell GPU at 6,144 CUDA cores, up to 128GB LPDDR5X, 180-200 AI TOPS) and DGX Station coming to Windows for trillion-parameter local inference. The day-of China comparison is the Spirit AI x D-Robotics RDK S600 stack, Spirit v1.5 ranked #1 on RoboChallenge at 560 TOPS on a single domestic SoC. The twist is that NVIDIA just stapled its edge silicon onto Chinese robot hardware, so the China-domestic-chip wedge now competes against a stack that has already absorbed the Chinese chassis.     Robotics & humanoids

    • H2 Plus is the most consequential humanoid hardware release of the quarter, and its design is a statement about who owns which layer. Unitree supplies the chassis (31 DOF), Sharpa supplies the dexterous hands (22 DOF, the single hardest unsolved hardware problem in manipulation), NVIDIA supplies the compute and the open software platform. Three vendors, three layers, one open reference design that any lab can buy. NVIDIA also confirmed it will build research humanoids with US, European and South Korean makers, so H2 Plus is the first reference unit, not the only one. The read for anyone selling a full vertically-integrated humanoid platform is that the research market just got a cheaper, open, multi-vendor alternative endorsed by the compute layer everyone depends on.     Research papers worth a look

    • TAVIS: A Benchmark for Egocentric Active Vision and Anticipatory Gaze in Imitation Learning (arXiv 2605.07943). Evaluation infrastructure for active-vision imitation learning, with task suites on two humanoid-torso embodiments (GR1T2, Reachy2). The relevant angle for a capture-side wedge is that it formalizes anticipatory gaze and active vision as things a dataset has to carry, not just hand and object trajectories. Where the camera looks and when it looks there becomes part of the spec. That is a yield-and-validation argument, not an hour-count one, and it is the direction the egocentric-capture literature keeps pushing. arXiv 2605.07943
    • NVIDIA Isaac GR00T Reference Humanoid Robot (open release, June 1). Not a paper, but the open hardware-plus-software reference design is the release that matters most this week. H2 Plus chassis plus Sharpa hands plus Jetson Thor plus the open Isaac GR00T platform, available October, adopted by Ai2, ETH Zurich, Stanford Robotics Center and UCSD ARCL. Track it as the embodiment that the next wave of academic VLA results will be benchmarked on. NVIDIA newsroom
