    Data collection

    • RoboDream is the collection-side signal today, and it cuts the way every world-model paper this spring has cut. It synthesizes demonstrations with novel objects, novel scenes and novel viewpoints by anchoring generation to rendered robot motion while conditioning on explicit scene and object priors, so trajectory execution is decoupled from environment synthesis. The real-world experiments show generated data lifting downstream policy performance and reducing how much real data a task needs. The capture-side read is the one we keep making. As synthetic and internet priors do more of the heavy lifting, raw hour count keeps getting re-priced, and what survives is coverage no public corpus holds plus validated yield against a spec, not undifferentiated volume. RoboDream still needs real trajectories to anchor on and real scene priors to condition with, which is exactly the validated, in-distribution input that does not commoditize. arXiv 2606.02577

    Research papers worth a look

    • RoboDream: Compositional World Models for Scalable Robot Data Synthesis (arXiv 2606.02577), submitted June 1, TRI and collaborators. An embodiment-centric world model that synthesizes photorealistic demonstrations with novel objects, scenes and viewpoints by anchoring on rendered robot motion and conditioning on scene and object priors. The two capabilities to note are “retrieval and rebirth,” which repurposes existing trajectories into new contexts with no new motion data, and “prop-free teleoperation,” where the operator manipulates empty air and the model fills in the objects and scene afterward, killing reset time. Real-world experiments show the generated data lifts policy performance and cuts how much real data each task needs. The capture-side point holds, this re-prices raw hours but still depends on real anchor trajectories and real scene priors, which is the validated in-distribution input worth owning. arXiv 2606.02577
